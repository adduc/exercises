# Exercises per project

Maybe it's a sign of age, a sign of experience, or perhaps just a new approach to work, but I find the quantity and quality of proof-of-concepts for projects has changed over the years.

At my first tech job, there were multiple projects I built and iterated on multiple times before shipping. Attributable to the combination of perfectionism and inexperience, these projects would work, but they would often be over-engineered, with functionality that wasn't needed or ended up needing to be redesigned later.

As I've gained more experience, I've found that I prefer to build more focused proof-of-concepts. I try to limit the scope of what I'm building to a single exercise or feature that can be completed in a short amount of time. This allows me to iterate quickly and get feedback sooner, rather than spending weeks or months on a project that may not even be used.
